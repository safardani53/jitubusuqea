Frank Oliver Howard, aka the Capital Punisher, was an American baseball player, coach, and manager in Major League Baseball.

He was born in Columbus, Ohio, to his parents, John and Erma Howard, as one of their six children.

After graduating from South High School, he attended Ohio State University, where he played baseball and basketball.

Although signed by the Philadelphia Warriors of the NBA, the 4x All-Star instead signed with the Los Angeles Dodgers.

Later, the 1963 World Series champion played with Washington Senators (now Texas Rangers), Detroit Tigers of MLB, and Taiheiyo Club Lions of Japan’s Pacific League.

Following his professional playing career, he turned coach for several MLB teams, including Milwaukee Brewers, New York Mets, Seattle Mariners, New York Yankees, and Tampa Bay Devil Rays.

Unfortunately, the Washington Nationals Ring of Honor award winner died on October 30, 2023.

Frank Howard Is Survived By His Extended Family: Obituary

Frank Howard passed away at the age of 87 on October 30, 2023, due to complications from a stroke.

He is remembered not only for his remarkable baseball career but also for his enduring family bonds.

The “Capital Punisher” had two marriages during his lifetime, first with Carol Johanski and second with Donna Howard.

Frank and Carol tied the knot in 1959; however, they parted ways in the mid-1980s.

Later, Frank married Donna Howard in 1990. Unfortunately, their marriage was cut short as Donna passed away on March 13, 2016, following an 18-month battle with cancer.

Following the death of his second wife, Frank and his first wife rekindled their relationship.

Frank Howard, the late baseball legend, was a devoted family man. He and Carol raised a loving family together, including six children: Catherine, Tim, Daniel, Mary, Mitch, and Rebecca.

In the wake of Frank’s passing, he is survived by his beloved wife, his cherished children, and eight adoring grandchildren, leaving behind a legacy of both sporting excellence and enduring family ties.

Frank Howard’s memory lives on in the hearts of his family and all those who admired his “Capital Punisher” legacy.
