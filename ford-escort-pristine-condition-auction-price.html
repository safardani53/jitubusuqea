 Posted on April 24, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  297 words  |  Larita Shotwell

A RARE convertible Ford Cortina in a pristine condition has hit auction for an eye-watering price.

The Escort XR3i Cabriolet travelled only 6,000 miles since 2006 - making it a highly desirable purchase.

The Ford's current highest bid is a whopping £6,750 - and with just 30 hours left on the auction, time is running out fast.

Auctioneers Car and Classic say the motor has avoided the "ravages of everyday abuse" and is currently in ideal condition.

The listing reads: "Clearly owned by Ford enthusiasts, this beautiful XR3i was treated to a meticulous overhaul around ten years ago and has only travelled just over 6,000 miles since 2006.

"The restoration included a full professional quality respray in Flambeau Red over Moonstone and a new soft-top outer skin.

"The front and rear suspension and brakes were renewed and factory specification features such as the RS spoiler and desirable Ford alloys remain - and look immaculate."

The mileage on the motors last MOT in 2006 was 72,147 - meaning it has travelled just 6,137 miles in the last 17 years.

Auctioneers add: "The XR3i cabriolet is a relative bargain today compared to the hatch, and with few remaining and interest in performance Ford’s rising, this rarity is without doubt an appealing proposition.

"By-passed by the boy-racers, this show-quality car has obviously been restored and cared for by Ford enthusiasts who know their stuff and should offer the new custodian the chance enjoy some sporty modern-classic open-top motoring."
