Terrified children sent screaming Shark as massive fish chases them from water in Brit hols ho

 Posted on June 21, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  442 words  |  Aldo Pusey

TERRIFIED kids were seen screaming "Shark" as a huge fish chased them from the waters at a popular Brit holiday hotspot.

The children ran for their lives when they spotted a dorsal fin emerging from the shallow waters in Malta.

The shocking incident took place on Melliena beach, a popular tourist hotspot in Ghadira Bay, on Wednesday June 21.

Footage of the chaotic scenes show the youngsters screaming and fleeing from the beast as it thrashed its powerful tail - but not everything was as it seemed.

Four children could be seen shouting: "Oh my god, run! Shark!" as the creature swam rapidly towards them.

The waters were bubbling and splashing from the animal's sharp movements as the kids frantically ran back towards the beach.

A lifeguard blew on a whistle and yelled at the tourists to get out of the water.

But the truth was soon revealed as locals quickly realised that what the kids had thought was a shark - was actually just a huge tuna fish.

A bloke could be heard shouting "tuna, tuna" to the horrified kids in an effort to reassure them that they weren't about to become shark food.

The footage ended with the tuna beginning to swim away after almost getting stuck in the shallow waters.

But unfortunately the fish didn't make it far as it got stuck on a shallow rocky part of the nearby shore.

It later emerged that the bluefin tuna had died after becoming stuck and fatally injuring itself on the sharp rocks.

Beach-goers tried to pull the tuna away from the deadly rocks but it was too late as the fish had already died.

Another piece of footage from the scene shows the bluefin lying still in the waters after its ferocious thrashing just a short while earlier.

As tourists dragged the body to shore, it could be seen littered with bloody cuts and puncture wounds.

It is currently unclear where the large tuna fish came from, with local media speculating that it might have escaped from a nearby fish farm.

The bluefin is the largest type of tuna in the world, measuring between six and 10 feet.

They can also weigh up to 1,500 pounds and live for 35 years on average.

Bluefin tuna's are predators, seeking out schools of fish such as herring, mackerel and eels.
