Don't Forget The Lyrics!: Game rules and prize

 Posted on June 25, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  408 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

DON'T Forget The Lyrics! is back in 2022 for a revival hosted by Niecy Nash.

The original game show ran from 2007 to 2009 and was hosted by Wayne Brady.

What are the rules of the show?

Don't Forget The Lyrics is a game show hosted on the Fox network.

Contestants have nine categories of songs to sing from, and must must pick two songs to sing, karaoke-style.

There is a screen helping the contest along - just like karaoke - until it begins to have blanks.

It is up to the contestant to fill in the blanks of the missing lyrics.

The host, Niecy Nash, can help them by telling them the number of words missing.

Along with the host's help, the contestant also has a "backup singer" such as a family member or friend to help them.

And they receive up to three words to use when they need them.

Once they lock in the lyrics and if they get them correct, the contestant can move up the money ladder.

What does the winner receive?

If the contestant locks in the lyric correctly, they will win the grand prize ranging from $25,000 to $1million.

If they complete the first nine categories, then they get the chance to sing for the $1million prize spot.

But they can stop and bag the money - as Fletch did in episode one when he walked away with $150,000 after completing eight categories.

Don't Forget The Lyrics is said to be a stylistic combination of the show's Name That Tune and Who Wants To Be A Millionaire?

The show premiered on May 23, 2022.

Fox announced Nash as the show host on April 28, 2022, via Twitter.

The account wrote: "Get ready for
@NiecyNash! Don't miss #DontForgetTheLyrics — a new musical series coming soon to @FOXTV."

Nash is a comedian and actress known for her roles in Claws, Reno 911, Reno 911! Miami, and more.

How can I watch Don't Forget The Lyrics?

Viewers can tune in to the show on Mondays on the Fox network.

Don't Forget The Lyrics is on at 8/7c.
