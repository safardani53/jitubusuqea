Tributes to couple, aged 35 and 54, found dead inside tattoo parlour in 'suspected murder-suicide'

 Posted on January 28, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  588 words  |  Martina Birk

TRIBUTES have poured in for a couple found dead in a tattoo parlour in a suspected murder-suicide.

Catalin Micu, 54, and Ramona Stoia, 35, died in GothInk Studio in Canterbury, Kent, around 5.20pm on Monday.

The couple - who shared a son - ran the studio together and were praised as "amazing artists" and an "absolutely lovely" pair.

GothInk Studio- less than a mile from the University for Creative Arts and a stone's throw from Canterbury Cathedral - will remain shut for several days, a statement said.

Cops confirmed the death of the 54-year-old man is not being treated as suspicious - but the woman's death is.

Officers are not looking for anyone else in connection with the investigation.

Tributes poured in for the pair online, with devastated customers thanking them for their work.

"I’ll never forget when I was in there for a six-hour tattoo and Ramona ran over and got me a McDonald’s, always so friendly, giving out sweets so I could sit out a full seven-hour tattoo.

"I’m gutted I won’t be able to get my tattoo from Ramona.

"My heart goes out to their son! RIP Catalin Micu & Ramona."

Forensic officers in hazmat suits were seen going in and out of a tattoo parlour on the busy street last night.

GothInk Studio- less than a mile from the University for Creative Arts and a stone's throw from Canterbury Cathedral - will remain shut for several days, a statement said.

Local business owners, who knew the "nice and quiet" couple, have spoken of their horror.

Owner of Omicron Print Specialists, Mark Fawcett-Jones, 47, said: “I was just closing up at around 5.15pm when it went crazy.

“There were loads of police and sirens."

He added: “It was probably a stabbing.

“The two people who owned the store were involved.

“They were a nice couple. They’ve been here for a few years. They were nice and quiet.”

Anggoon Buttayotee, owner of nearby Oriental Grocers, added: “The police kept coming and coming. But we don’t know details.”

Matthew Sandhu, 27, who helps run The Pilgrim's Shop, a newsagents, said he was familiar with Catalin and Ramona, and spoke of his shock at the incident.

He added: “I’ve seen them around and didn’t think they were any trouble.

“It’s crazy, you see it all over the news all the time, but when it happens around here you think oh s***.”

Cops said inquiries into their deaths are ongoing - but have confirmed they are not looking for anyone else.

A Kent Police spokesperson said: "At 5.19pm on Monday 11 April 2022, officers attended an address in Lower Bridge Street, Canterbury, where a man and a woman, who were known to each other, were pronounced deceased at the scene.

"Following enquiries by detectives from the Kent and Essex Serious Crime Directorate, the death of the 54-year-old man is not being treated as suspicious.

"The death of the 35-year-old woman is currently being treated as suspicious, however officers are not looking for anyone else in connection with the investigation.
