Five bottled beverages you'll never drink again - and fans are upset

 Posted on August 4, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  876 words  |  Martina Birk

THERE are several beloved bottled beverages that made their mark on Americans' taste buds but unfortunately will never see a return to stores.

Snapple has been an iconic beverage to drink on the go since it first launched its glass bottles in the early 1970s.

While today the brand carries plastic bottles instead of its former glass flavors, the beverage has maintained a special place in many Americans’ hearts.

That doesn’t mean the company hasn’t experienced significant changes in its product lineup over the years.

While Snapple Apple arguably remains the most popular of all Snapple flavors, fans are always excited to try various new creative flavors from the brand.

Unfortunately, not all of them are hits, and several were discontinued before they ever found proper footing over the years.

Whether customers saw these flavors as huge flops or are actually begging the company to bring them back, these are some of the most famous discontinued Snapples over the years.

1. LIME GREEN TEA

Green Tea Snapples have claimed the hearts of Americans everywhere, but once upon a time, the brand actually tried its hand at a more citrusy version of the beverage.

The drink first made an appearance in the 2000s and was a stark contrast to the original Green Tea flavor.

There was a much higher proportion of lime than tea, and past reviewers said it was hard to note any green tea flavor at all.

This could have led to the product’s discontinuation since the taste didn’t actually match what the beverage advertised.

2. SAMOAN SPLASH

Some Snapple fans adored the Samoan Splash flavor as it brought back memories of island getaways and vacations yet to come.

The exact flavor profile gave hints of orange, banana, strawberry, and cupuaçu.

However, as one of Snapple’s original Island Cocktail line of flavors, it did not appear to draw enough fans for it to stay around long.

While strawberry and banana make a compelling flavor duo, not many Americans even knew what the Brazilian cupuaçu fruit even was.

This lack of awareness might have prevented some shoppers from getting on board with the flavor.

Those who did try the Samoan Splash compared it to a basic strawberry and banana smoothie, so Snapple fans looking for a more tropical taste might have been disappointed too.

3. SNAPPLE SODAS

Back in the day, you could actually drink a beverage that was designed to perfectly blend Snapples and sodas together.

The brand decided to expand beyond its iced tea and juice flavors to launch a line of caffeine-free sodas in the 1980s.

Some of the most famed flavors included Tru Root Beer, Peach Melba, French Cherry, Cream D’Vanilla, Passion Fruit, Chocolate, and Cherry-Lime Rickey.

While the new creations made quite a splash in the drink scene when they first came out, they were all but gone by the mid-1990s.

Many fans are hoping the line could make a comeback, but Snapple has been quiet on the matter.

“On a fine spring day like this one, seems a shame to not be able to pop open a Snapple Cherry-Lime Rickey and quench that thirst with a tart and sweet Rickey wash,” a loyal fan wrote of the discarded Snapple sodas in The Dallas Observer.

4. GUAVA MANIA

Another tropical Snapple flavor that haunts loyal fans is Guava Mania.

The blend was initially popular in the 1990s when shoppers enjoyed the refreshing and sweet pink juice.

It gave a flavor profile of guava, kiwi, and strawberry, but unfortunately, not all things are meant to last.

Snapple has mostly abandoned guava as a flavor component in its drinks now.

However, some shoppers recall the drink being one of the more unique options available and they want it back.

"Hey, @Snapple whose idea it was to get rid of Guava Mania Cocktail, FIRE THEM IMMEDIATELY," a Twitter user wrote to the company.

5. SUMMER PEACH

Nothing hit perfectly on a warm, beautiful day like Snapple’s Summer Peach drink.

Fans loved it for its citrusy orange flavor combined with the sweet deliciousness of peaches. 

The color of the beverage alone could beckon you away into memories of a gorgeous beach sunset.

While many fans have asked Snapple if it’s possible to bring the beloved drink back, it does not seem likely at this point.

"Unfortunately, Summer Peach has been discontinued. However, we'll be sure to pass along the demand to our team," Snapple wrote on Twitter to one customer earnestly asking for the beverage to make a comeback.

Some fans have recommended the Summer Peach lemonade from Calypso as a worthy substitute, however.

"Idk if ppl remember the summer peach Snapple, but it was my favorite thing ever as a kid,” one fan tweeted. “It only came out during the summer months and I was starting to think I imagined it lmao.
