 Posted on September 7, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  823 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

KIM Kardashian has not impressed fans at all with her latest Instagram look.

The reality superstar modelled an unusual silver strapless dress which featured cut-out detailing on the bust, fell to the floor, and highlighted her slender frame.

Kim, 41, teamed the strapless garment with a silver quilted shawl which fell below her waist, and wore her blonde hair down in a loose wave.

The third image on the post was of a chunky silver armchair, presumably in her Los Angeles home.

Not all of Kim's followers were so keen on her futuristic attire and when one person re-shared it on Reddit, the thread was inundated with comments.

"I hate this outfit," one said bluntly, while someone else claimed: "This is the type of style that we are going to look back and cringe upon."

A third asked: "Was she inspired to wear the aluminum foil dress by the metal chair? (sic)."

Another person said: "I hope she’s just making fun of herself and not hinting that she’s going to release furniture," to which another Reddit user replied: "I think that’s exactly what she was doing. Her lack of a caption just didn’t drive the joke through."

A different person noted: "That piece of furniture looks very uncomfortable."

Another harsh critic commented: "Kim has some style, but she is not creative at all."

However, other fans praised Kim for her latest look and described it as "stunning" and "beautiful".

"Hottest thing under the sun," one of her Instagram followers wrote.

"So beautiful," a second wrote, while a third simply added: "I love it."

Kim's latest post came hours after fans of The Kardashians slammed her for some comments she made in the latest episode of the Hulu series.

Episode four of season two saw Kim get upset over the backlash she received over something she previously said to Variety magazine about women not working hard enough.

Earlier this year, the mom-of-four came under fire when she gave some pretty harsh advice during an interview and cover shoot with the publication.

At the time, the Keeping Up With the Kardashians alum said: “I have the best advice for women in business. Get your f**king a** up and work.”

“It seems like nobody wants to work these days," Kim continued

After her mom Kris Jenner and sister Kourtney Kardashian agreed with Kim’s statement, she added: “You have to surround yourself with people that want to work.

“Have a good work environment where everyone loves what they do because you have one life.

“No toxic work environments and show up and do the work.”

The latest episode of The Kardashians saw Kim fret about the backlash she had received over the comments - but she refused to apologize and said she had simply been misunderstood.

During a lunch date with her other sisters, Khloe Kardashian and Kendall Jenner, Kim said she was “usually so together” but was feeling “tested” lately.

“There’s just been so much going on because of that Variety interview, and it’s like I said what I said about people working," she explained to her siblings.

“Do I believe in that? Yes. I believe that you have to work hard in order to do that, but I was just so blindsided by how angry people got.”

She added: “Enough people were triggered so I want to be responsible and understand why people feel the way that they do and I do understand that.

“That some people aren’t given the same opportunities as us.”

KIM'S INSPIRATION

Kim then appeared to try and justify her comments and admitted that watching her momager Kris Jenner juggle work with raising a family had inspired her.

"But I also feel like if people understood, that like yeah we grew up privileged and people don’t know the story of your [Kendall’s] dad [Caitlyn Jenner] and mom having to sell their house in the Hills because they couldn’t afford it and they had to move to an apartment," she explained to her two sisters.

“And mom really started her career making money at 50, so it’s like growing up and seeing her hustle and seeing her get up every day with all these kids – six of hers that she had to take care of, I saw her work her a*s off and figure it out, get an agent’s license, get a manager license to figure out how to represent your dad and that really inspired me how to work," the Skims founder added.

And while discussing the reaction to her comments, Kim said in a confessional: “I think everyone would assume I got everything handed to me, being on a TV show.
