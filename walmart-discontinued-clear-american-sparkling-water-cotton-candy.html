Walmart shoppers 'very upset' over loss of discontinued candy-flavored drink which will never return

 Posted on August 9, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  328 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

SHOPPERS have expressed their anger on social media after a popular beverage was discontinued at Walmart.

The chain sells many types of sparkling water under the Clear American brand.

Until recently, the collection included a cotton variety of the seltzer.

But customers can no longer find it on the retailer's shelves.

"Walmart I am very upset that you have discontinued or that the brand Clear American has discontinued the Cotton Candy Sparkling Water," one Twitter user said.

"I want it back!"

Walmart's customer service account replied with a link to a page where patrons can provide the company with feedback.

"We suggest submitting a ticket here," the message said.

But shoppers still have many other varieties of Clear American sparkling water to choose from.

The company has flavors like blackberry lemonade, white grape, and cherry limeade available on its website.

There are 33-ounce bottles for sale starting at just 57 cents and 12-packs of cans going for as little as $2.98.

"Whether you are looking for the perfect drink to accompany your meal or want to try something new, this sparkling water is wonderful for both enjoying on its own or using as the main ingredient in a homemade punch," a product description on Walmart's website says.

Those still looking for a cotton candy-flavored drink at Walmart have a few options.

The company has 16-ounce cans of cotton candy Bucked Up Big Game Energy Drinks on sale for $2.48.

Each can has 150mg of caffeine.

Walmart also sells boutique bottles of cotton candy-flavored soda from Rocket Fizz for $10, down from the original price of $11.63.

Those looking to stock up can also get a 24-bottle pack of cotton candy-flavored pop from the iconic cult brand Faygo.

This flavor of sparkling is far from the first product that's been discontinued at Walmart.

The company has also stopped selling many beloved kitchenware items compiled in this list by The U.S. Sun.
