Coffee-Mate confirms it has discontinued popular creamer fans say they miss it immensely'

 Posted on October 7, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  501 words  |  Aldo Pusey

COFFEE-Mate has discontinued a popular creamer – but that isn't stopping fans from begging for a comeback.

The Nestle-owned brand is known for making various flavors to sweeten up your morning.

There are some classics including French vanilla that will likely not disappear anytime soon.

Others, meanwhile, may get launched for a limited time and never make an appearance again.

But one fan has asked about the availability of the Cookies 'N Cocoa flavor, which was rumored to be removed from the grocery store shelves.

Well, Coffee-Mate in response to the customer on Twitter said: "That flavor has been discontinued, but we can make a note that you miss it!"

And the fan in response tweeted back with weeping face emoji: "Aw man okay I hope it comes back soon."

The Cookies 'N Cocoa creamer consisted of seed oils, natural and artificial flavors, and carrageenan.

Also, it had five grams of added sugar.

Previously, the creamer was sold in a 32-fluid-ounce container for $4.69 at Kroger.

Keep in mind that prices can vary by location.

OTHER ALTERNATIVES TO BUY

While nothing might compare to the Cookies 'N Cocoa flavor from Coffee-Mate, there might be some similar flavors in the grocery store that you'll want to consider.

For example, Walmart sells a Speculoos Cookie creamer under its Great Value brand for $2.68 in a 32-fluid-ounce container.

It is described as having a "caramelized sugar cookie taste," according to the product details.

Also, some may want to try a flavor by Coffee-Mate flavor that is inspired by a popular Kellogg's treat.

Its Rice Krispies Treats variety sells for $4.26 in a 32-ounce container at Walmart.

OTHER DISCONTINUED PRODUCTS

But Coffee-Mate has more bad news for fans.

That's because the company has confirmed that its Chocolate Raspberry flavor is gone too.

It was actually axed before 2012 – but fans have been begging for it to come back.

Another coffee creamer brand, International Delight, has discontinued its Cold Stone Creamery flavor.

The creamer was both gluten- and lactose-free, which made it available to a wide range of coffee drinkers.

Another brand, Gatorade, has axed its Blueberry Pomegranate flavor.

The flavor was described on the label as a "low-calorie thirst quencher" option, which is what all of its G2 varieties aim to bring to the table.

Its ingredients consist of artificial flavoring and coloring, as well as sugar and sucralose.

Coca-Cola said that it has stopped producing its orange vanilla soda.

The company offered a sugar-free variety of this flavor as well.

“Coca-Cola Orange Vanilla Zero Sugar was discontinued so that we can focus on flavors that show the most potential for growth and scale," the brand said in response to a fan that asked about the orange vanilla soda.
