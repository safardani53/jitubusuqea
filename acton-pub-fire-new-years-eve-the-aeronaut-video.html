Acton pub fire sees hundreds evacuated as massive blaze rips through New Year's Eve party at The Aer

 Posted on September 30, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  590 words  |  Martina Birk

EIGHT people have been rescued after a pub went up in flames just after midnight during a New Year's party.

More than 340 people and 12 staff rushed to escape the blaze with eight people, including five police officers, having to be treated for minor injuries after the fire broke out at the Aeronaut pub in Acton just after 12.30am on January 1.

One woman had to be dragged from the burning building by PC Ben Jacques after she suffered from an asthma attack from the heavy smoke.

Another man who suffered serious cuts to his hand after desperately trying to smash a window to escape also had to rescued.

Eight people were rescued from the first floor thanks to a ladder.

In a video of the terrifying fire, windows can be heard smashing as the flames take hold of the pub.

Police can be seen waving away the crowds as the blaze takes hold of the pub.

The first and second floors and the roof of the three storey building were gutted by the blaze while  half of its ground floor was also left badly damaged.

Authorities are now left to investigate the cause of the blaze while the pub owners count their blessings that no one was badly hurt.

Musician Matt Blair shared his terror on social media, writing on Twitter: "We're fine but 2017 has already got off to a bad start. One of our favourite venues that we just played burned down. Everyone is fine."

He added: "Manic. We were upstairs, had to get on a roof area and luckily found a ladder we could climb down. Scary stuff."

The pub shared a message on their website after the blaze, saying: "As you might have heard our New Year has started in a very upsetting way, but we’re also counting our blessings.

"Just 30 minutes after the countdown The Aeronaut caught fire, but thanks to the quick thinking of our staff, and the evacuation plans they have had in place over the years, everyone got out the building safely. That’s the main thing, and everything else comes second."

The pub also shared their thanks for the emergency services who rushed in to help extinguish the blaze.

They said: "The building we love so much has been extinguished by the incredible firemen who acted so quickly. At this point we don’t really know how bad that damage is, but for the moment we’re concentrating on people.

"We will never be able to fully articulate our gratitude for the swift action of the police and fire services in managing the incident and for the many words of support we have received from the Acton community."

London Fire Brigade said that ten fire engines and 72 firefighters rushed to the scene.

Acton Police Station, which is next door, was evacuated during the fire.

Chief Superintendent Paul Martin, Borough Commander for Ealing, said: "I'm proud of the bravery and determination shown by my officers who went into a burning smoke filled pub to help rescue those inside and ensure an orderly evacuation.

"The damage inside is extensive. Thankfully no one has been seriously injured and that is largely thanks to the efforts of police who acted decisively in the face of danger.

"I'd like to wish all those injured, including the five officers who suffered smoke inhalation, a speedy recovery."

There is nothing to suggest that the cause of the fire was suspicious.
