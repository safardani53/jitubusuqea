Kirsten Dunst, born April 30, 1982, is an American actress. Kirsten began her career as a child actress, appearing in such films as ‘High Strung,’ Jumanji,’ and ‘Little Women.’ In 1999, Kirsten began her long collaboration with Oscar-winning director Sofia Coppola by appearing in Sofia’s first film ‘The Virgin Suicides.’ The independent film was lauded by critics, and Kirsten went on to collaborate with Sofia on projects like ‘Marie Antoinette’ and ‘The Beguiled.’ 

Kirsten is also widely known among comic book fans for her portrayal of Mary Jane Watson in the early 2000s ‘Spider-Man’ trilogy opposite Toby Maguire. The actress has also established a fine balance of work in film and TV, appearing in the FX series ‘Fargo’ for which she earned an Emmy nomination in 2016. 

The actress has earned wide acclaim for her work over the course of her career, earning nominations from the Hollywood Foreign Press Association, TV Academy, Cannes Film Festival, and various critics organizations. Kirsten has been in a relationship with her ‘Fargo’ co-star Jesse Plemons since 2017. They have one child together.
