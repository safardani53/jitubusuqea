Barbara Ann Cochran is a former alpine ski athlete at the World Cup and won the US Olympic gold medal. Born in Claremont, New Hampshire, Cochran is the second of four siblings of the famous "Snowman" family of Richmond, Vermont, who have been running a small ski resort in their backyard since five 1961. Her father, Gordon "Mickey" Cochran, is a senior coach, coaching teenagers with the Smuggler's Notch Ski Club, the University of Vermont Ski Team and the American Ski Team. The family has brought generations of athletes to the US ski team: sister Marilyn, Barbara Ann, brother Bob 9 times national champions and sister twice national champions Lindy. The next generation of the family includes niece Jessica Kelley, nephews Jimmy Cochran, Roger Brown, Tim Kelley, Robby Kelley and his son, Ryan Cochran-Siegle. After retiring from football, Cochran graduated from the University of Vermont, married Ron Williams and published the book Skiing for Women.
