Early Life, Family & Education

Royce Khalil O’Neale was born to his mother, Deborah Kingwood, and father, Roy O’Neale, on June 5, 1993, in Killeen, Texas.

No information about his siblings is available. Either O’Neale does not have any, or he just does not want to share information about them with the media. Whatever may be the case, we fully respect his decisions.

The NBA star got a basketball as a gift on his 2nd birthday and loved to watch basketball games. He started playing club basketball when he was in 3rd grade.

Moving on to his education, Royce went to Harker Heights High School, which is located in Texas. He had an exceptional high school career and later got his jersey ’00’ retired.

Upon graduation, he went to the University of Denver after getting recruited under a basketball scholarship.

However, he only played two seasons at Denver before transferring to Baylor University, where he was closer to his home and family.

Career & Stats

After not getting picked by any teams in the 2015 NBA draft, Royce decided that he needed to go to another continent in order to make a living.

Hence, he moved to Germany, where he played for MHP Riesen Ludwigsburg. During his one season, Royce averaged 8.1 points and 4.5 rebounds.

After that, he played for Herbalife Gran Canaria and Zalgiris Kaunas before achieving his dreams of playing in the NBA in 2017.

However, even before signing for the Utah Jazz, O’Neale had already got a taste of the NBA when he got to play in the Summer League for the Golden State Warriors in 2016.

As a result, Royce found it relatively easy to adapt to the best basketball league in the world, the NBA.

NBA

During his first season at Utah, O’Neale managed to play in 80 games, including the playoffs, averaging 6.1 points, 1.4 assists, and 3.5 rebounds per game.

Thus, having performed brilliantly in his rookie season, everyone expected the Texas native to perform well.

And that is precisely what Royce did as he played in 82 games averaging 5.2 points, 3.5 rebounds, and 1.5 assists per game in his regular season.

Moreover, his playoff performances during the 2019 playoffs were so impressive that the Jazz front office decided to hand him a new four-year deal in 2020.

Since the 2018-19 season, Royce’s performance has gradually improved as he averaged 6.3, 7.0, and 7.4 points per game in the 2019-20, 2020-21, and 2021-22 seasons, respectively.

After his impressive performance with the Utah Jazz, on June 30, 2022, O’Neale was traded to the Brooklyn Nets in exchange for a 2023 first-round draft pick.

So far in the 2022-23 season, the small forward has played 47 games, starting in all of them, averaging 9.5 points, 4.9 rebounds, and 4.0 assists.

O’Neale was traded to the Brooklyn Nets in exchange for a 2023 first-round draft pick. He put up his first career triple-double in a 109-107 win over the Portland Trail Blazers on November 17, 2022.

He registered a game-winning putback in a win over the Miami Heat on January 8, 2023.

Net Worth & Salary

As of 2023, Royce has a net worth of $2 million, thanks to his career as an NBA player.

However, most people might be compelled to think that Royce’s net worth is much lower as compared to other players who earn a similar sum as him.

During his time with the Utah Jazz, the Texas native earned around $21 million in 5 seasons.

With the Nets, his annual salary is estimated to be around $9 million while carrying a cap hit of $9.2 million.

Contract

On January 19, 2020, Royce signed a new 4-year/$36 million contract extension after three commendable seasons with the Utah Jazz.

As per the contract, O’Neale earned $8.5 million in the 2020-21 season and $8.8 million in the 2021-22 season.
