Where to Stream Elf this Holiday Season

 Posted on May 9, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  406 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

You don't have to travel through the seven layers of the Candy Cane Forest to watch this holiday classic!

Elf, starring Will Ferrell and Zooey Deschanel, is celebrating its 20th anniversary this year following its initial release on Nov. 7, 2003.

Two decades later, the Christmas comedy has remained a seasonal staple for families due to its star-studded cast, mix of silliness and sentimentality, and of course, Buddy the Elf's iconic quotes throughout the film.

Elf was a box office hit, grossing $223 million worldwide and becoming an instant Christmas staple. For many fans of the film, the holiday hit continues to be watched year-round, whether it's the yuletide season or not.

Read on for how to stream Elf now!

What is Elf about?

If smiling is your favorite, this 2003 comedy is the perfect film to stream!

The film follows Will Ferrell's Buddy the Elf as he travels to New York City to find his biological father after having been raised in the North Pole by Santa's elves. While in the Big Apple, he helps his family and others rediscover the origins of Christmas.

Zooey Deschanel, Ed Asner, James Caan and Bob Newhart are among the other stars in the film. So, make yourself a bowl of maple syrup-drenched spaghetti and feast your eyes on Elf.

Where to can Elf be streamed?

There are various ways to stream Elf online. The Warner Bros. Discovery-owned streaming service, Max, service offers Elf as part of its library, that isn't the only platform on which you can watch it. 

Elf is available to view on Apple TV, Amazon Prime Video, YouTube and Vudu, too. However, each requires a rental purchase.

Is there an Elf sequel?

An Elf sequel has topped fans' Christmas lists since the film premiered two decades ago. However, their wishes have yet to be granted.

Elf casting director, Susie Farris, recently told PEOPLE she would "love to" work on a remake if one ever came to fruition, but it's unlikely to happen with Ferrell back in the famous tights.

"I've read that Will felt like he gave this character all that he could give it," Farris said. "I think if Will wanted to do a sequel, there would be a sequel. Creatively, I hope that he is very fulfilled in life, but I'd like him to do this."
