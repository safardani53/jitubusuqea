Dwight Powell | Early Life & Family

Dwight Powell was born and raised in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. His parents are Harlan Powell and Jacqueline Weir.

Furthermore, the athlete’s parents split up when he was very young. They were never married, and he was their only child.

Aside from that, the NBA player is very close to his mother. Jacqueline was a bank executive and a very caring and loving mother.

Furthermore, she worked hard to provide her son with the best life that he could have.

Similarly, she taught him the importance of academics and always supported his love for basketball.

Besides that, the power forward’s mother cared more for her son’s future, well-being, and happiness. So, she didn’t utter a word when she was diagnosed with breast cancer. 

Weir wanted Powell to focus on his collegiate Career and not worry about her.

Only when the power forward visited his mother’s Boston house; did he realize how sick she was.

By then, cancer had spread to her liver and only worsened. Jacqueline was getting weaker every day.

As a result, she passed away on September 13, 2012. The player was utterly heartbroken over the loss of his mother.

Nevertheless, he kept her in his heart and played to his full potential. Dwight honored his mother’s wish by playing the best he could.

Dwight Powell | High School And College Career

Powell has been fond of basketball ever since he was a kid. His passion and love for the game only grew over time.

Initially, he attended Earl Haig High School to play basketball for Earl Haig Carp. However, he wasn’t getting any recognition from recruiters in Toronto.

As a result, he attended a preparatory boarding school, IMG Academy, in Bradenton, Florida.

The academy helped the NBA player sharpen his basketball skills. Moreover, he improved overall as a player.

As a result, Dwight had an exceptional senior year. Furthermore, Rivals.com named him a five-star recruit.

 Following his high school graduation, the power forward chose to attend Stanford University to continue his basketball journey. 

He played 31 games and started 26 of them. Correspondingly, he received the Pac-10 All-Freshman Team honors.

Besides that, he helped the Stanford Cardinals win the National Invitation Tournament Championship.

He also obtained a Pac-12 All-Academic Honorable Mention selection as a sophomore.

In his junior year, the athlete lost his mother to breast cancer. However, her demise fueled him to fulfill her wish by playing his best.

Hence, he received second-team NABC All-District, first-team All-Pac-12, and Pac-12 All-Academic second-team honors.

On top of that, the basketballer was the Pac-12 Most Improved Player of the Year.

In addition, the Maverick power forward received All-Pac-12 honors for the second time in his senior year.

Likewise, he was the Pac-12 Scholar-Athlete of the Year, NCAA All-South Regional Team, and Pac-12 All-Academic Honorable Mention selection.

After the 2014 season, the power forward was traded to the Dallas Mavericks. In his rookie year, Powell was assigned to the Texas Legends frequently.

As a result, he re-signed with the Mavericks for four years in a contract worth over $37 million.

After that, he set new career highs and assisted the team with phenomenal wins.

Dwight also played alongside and built a good relationship with one of the best power forward, Dirk Nowitzki.

Although he wanted to explore other teams after the 2019 season, the athlete signed an extension contract with the Mavs.

He has the quickness, size, and athleticism to get to the rim quickly and finish with a top-level rate.

Likewise, he has the offensive efficiency ability to get to the line and knockdown.

His high energy and aggressive rim-attacking style go a long way in putting foul pressure on the opponent and getting easy points for his team.

All in all, he is one of the most efficient offensive players in the league.

Furthermore, the deal is extended for three years and is worth over $33 million. Dwight will become an unrestricted free agent in 2023.
