REBIRTHING is an experiential therapy made famous in the 1960s and 1970s by holistic healer Leonard Orr.

It is also considered experimental and isn't backed by any significant scientific body.

What is rebirthing?

Rebirthing is a type of therapy that utilizes breathwork and other methods to help the patient remember the trauma of their birth, as well as any traumas that they underwent in early childhood.

The main method taught by Orr is one that includes breathing in and out continuously, without stopping to pause.

The breathwork session itself can last for up to two hours and is said to help the patients to tap into their cellular memory and unearth any memories of childhood trauma that their conscious minds may have been blocking.

This practice should only be tried with a well-known therapist who has regard for when breathwork can become dangerous and is CPR certified and ready to end a session if an emergency happens.

Does rebirthing involve actually feeling reborn?

Reportedly, rebirthing leaves patients feeling at peace and relaxed, and some claim it helps with mental health, to resolve lingering effects of attachment disorders that stem from early childhood.

“They breathed themselves out of pain and tension into relaxation and peace," reported Orr.

"Most Rebirthing Breathwork sessions are physical, emotional, and spiritual."

"People experience breathing out pain, tension, drama, and trauma into relaxation and peace.”

Some therapists actually have their patients in a womb-like structure, such as a sleeping bag wrapped tightly in blankets or in a hot tub, to re-create the feeling of being expelled from the womb.

Whether they go through a physical exercise to re-create the sensations of the womb is up to the therapist and the patient.

According to Orr's work, the rebirth process should be able to take place with only breathwork.

What types of disorders is rebirthing good for?

The most well-known disorder that rebirthing is recommended for is those that are an outcome of having survived reactive attachment disorder.

Reactive attachment disorder is when children cannot form healthy relationships with parental figures or other children due to abandonment, abuse, or other forms of trauma during their early childhood.

It is considered to be a childhood disorder that children eventually grow out of.

Adults who have gone through early childhood trauma do not have the same diagnosis, but they may have some symptoms left over from their traumas, including:

  • anxiety
  • PTSD
  • depression
  • unhealthy relationships
  • lack of self-esteem
  • inability to control emotions
  • lack of interest in being social
  • self-harm
  • substance abuse
