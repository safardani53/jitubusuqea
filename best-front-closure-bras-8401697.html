Things to Consider When Shopping for Front Closure Bras

Closure Type 

When it comes to front closure bras, the type of closure is a big deal. You've got options, from zippers to snaps (magnetic) to barrels, according to bra-fitting expert Katie Weir. However, when in doubt, she recommends sticking with barrel closure bras as they provide extra security. “Opt for a barrel closure as this will give more security and ensure the bra doesn’t pop open which can happen with magnet closure styles,” she tells PEOPLE. 

Fit 

As Weir points out, it's important to make sure a front-closure bra fits perfectly, which goes for all bras. “As with any bra, we want to make sure the breast tissue is contained within the cups without falling into the center of the bra,” she says. "We also want breast separation and no breasts rubbing together.”

Materials

When it comes to finding the best front-closure bras, materials are also an important consideration, as Sena states that since the bra needs to meet in the middle, you want a fabric that offers good stretch. “I would look for a microfiber with a higher percentage of elastane (microfiber is a blend of nylon for softness and elastane for stretch, and bras all contain different amounts of it),” she says. The Playtex 18 Hour Front-Close Wirefree Bra is one example of a microfiber bra on this list.

Support 

Just like any other bra, support is key in a front closure bra, says Sena. “Support should always be the biggest consideration when purchasing any bra,” she explains. “For a bra with a front closure, you should look for some structure in the cups, so foam cups and an underwire are a good idea. Keep in mind that the band is not adjustable so you want to make sure the fit is perfect.”

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is the point of a front closure bra?

    As previously stated by Sena, front closure bras are designed primarily for people who have mobility and dexterity issues with their arms, hands, and shoulders, and who cannot clasp a back close bra. These bras have a hook and eye closure at the front, making them easy to fasten and undo when you're done wearing the bra.

  • Who should wear a front closure bra?

    Anyone can wear a front closure bra, but Sena says but they are especially beneficial for those who struggle with back closures. “If you have issues getting your bra on in the morning because of a back closure, you can try this instead,” she says. “Or if you’ve recently had surgery and are sore and tender, this will make it quicker and easier to put on your bra.” 

    Breastfeeding women and women with wide-set breasts can also benefit from wearing a front-closure bra, Sena adds. “Nursing women could opt for a front closure bra since they can pop the clasp open with one hand and let baby feed," she explains. "It can also be great for women with splayed or wide-set breasts as it can essentially push the breasts together to create cleavage without a ton of padding or push-up.”

  • What are the disadvantages of front open bras?

    Sena explains that while front closure bras are easy to put on and take off, they do come with some disadvantages. “If the bra doesn't fit perfectly, you might experience spillage since there's no room to play with in the front of the bra,” she tells PEOPLE. “Additionally, you’ll want to keep in mind that you won't have the same adjustability as a back close [bra] which usually has three or four rows of hook and eye closure.”
