Lidl launches dupes of designer Le Creuset cookware and prices start from just 3.99

 Posted on September 18, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  313 words  |  Aldo Pusey

DESIGNER cookware brand Le Creuset is the creme de la creme of pots and pans, but it comes with a hefty pricetag. 

Some of their classic dishes cost more than £200, but if you wanted a slice of luxury in your kitchen for a fraction of the price, check out Lidl’s new range. 

The budget supermarket has just unveiled a new cookware and crockery range, and the best part is theirs starts from just £3.99. 

Lidl has a range of colours on offer, just like its pricier rival, including bright yellow, deep red, teal and pale green. 

A cast iron casserole dish from the supermarket will set you back £24.99, when compared to Le Creuset’s £210 version. 

For that price you could pick yourself up an entire set from Lidl, with their stoneware oven dish costing just £4.99, while a frying pan will set you back £14.99, with a mini-version the cheapest in the range at £3.99. 

A two-pack of plates, bowls or mugs costs £4.99 each, while a triple pack of insulated bowls comes with a £12.99 pricetag.

And the supermarket is also selling brightly-coloured essentials, with a water bottle and food storage container set both on sale for £3.99. 

Lidl described their colourful kitchen must-haves as the “ideal accessories for any kitchen, whether for entertaining friends and family or having a chilled night in”. 

The limited-edition range hits shelves on Friday, but the supermarket warned once they’re gone, they’re gone for good.
