Who is Nisha Katona's husband Zoltan?

 Posted on October 3, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  341 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

NISHA Katona is due to be part of the judging panel for the 2022 series of the Great British Menu.

With her husband being a critically acclaimed musician, the pair have worked hard to get to where they are in their careers today.

Who is Nisha Katona's husband Zoltan?

Zoltan Katona is one half of the guitar duo The Katona Twins.

He was born in Budapest, Hungary, alongside his identical brother Peter in 1968.

The pair started playing music aged 10 and continued to do so throughout their teenage years.

He and his brother studied in various European cities including the Bela Bartok Conservatory of Music in Budapest, the Frankfurt University of Music and the Royal Academy of Music in London.

When did Nisha and Zoltan get married?

Nisha met Zoltan while she read law at Liverpool John Moores University.

The pair are married but they have kept the date of their marriage under wraps.

The couple lives in Wirral, near Liverpool.

How many children do Nisha and Zoltan Katona have?

Nisha and Zoltan are parents to two daughters, Tia and India.

The ages of the girls are unknown but in an interview conducted by Great British Life in 2021, they are both described as teenagers.

What are The Katona twins famous for?

The Katona Twins are famous for their guitar music.

The identical twins are a guitar duo and have played at some of the most prestigious venues, including the Royal Festival Hall and Wigmore Hall in London.

Their music style includes Spanish guitar, Classical and Tango.

The pair have won various awards including the Borletti-Buitoni prize in 2004, the first guitarists in the history of the trust to do so.

They have released ten albums in total, Hollywood & Swing was their latest one in 2020.

To celebrate their 30th anniversary, the duo held a special concert at Liverpool's St George's Hall Concert Room on June 11, 2019.

On June 19, 2022, The Katona Twins are due to play at the music festival in Thaxted.
