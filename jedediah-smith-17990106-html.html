Jedediah Strong Smith, is an American secretary, transcontinental pioneer, pioneer, hunter, trapper, author, map maker and Rocky Mountains explorer, Northwest American and Southwest American early 19th century. After 75 years of being obscured after his death, Smith was rediscovered as an American whose expeditions led to using the 20-mile-wide South Pass as a crossing point over the Continental Divider. for the Oregon pioneers on the Trail. Coming from a humble family, Smith moved to Saint-Louis and joined William H. Ashley and Andrew Henry's fur business in 1822. Smith directed the first recorded expedition from the Salt Lake border to the Colorado River. From there, Smith's group became the first US citizen to cross the Mojave Desert to reach what is today California, but at that time part of Mexico.
