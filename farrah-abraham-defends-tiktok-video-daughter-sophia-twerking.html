FORMER Teen Mom star Farrah Abraham is defending her 10-year-old daughter Sophia’s twerking video despite having reservations about posting the controversial clip.

Farrah, 28, exclusively revealed to The Sun that she posted the video to allow her daughter to “be a child”.

The ex-reality star came under fire for her parenting when she posted the video to her TikTok account earlier this month.

She told The Sun at ThinkBIG 2020 Conference presented by the Nazarian Institute, featuring host Dr. Sheila S. Nazarian on Saturday, January 11: “I said, ‘I don’t think that’s age appropriate because you’re twerking your booty’ and she’s like 'mom but all the kids are doing it.’

“I’m like, 'Okay I’ll try this for you, but I’m really not about it.'”

Farrah’s initial fears were validated when she received negative feedback on the video, as she received, “All bad things.”

But Farrah didn’t agree with the backlash, as she defended her decision.

She said: “It’s sad that thousands and millions of other kids can act like kids and Sophia doesn’t get that attribute of acting like a child.

"I feel like I’m allowing my kid to be a child and that’s first and foremost no. 1.”

In the video, Farrah danced as Sophia ran behind her mom and twerked.

One viewer commented: “Someone take her kid. CPS where you at?”

A second follower wrote: “Farrah keep your daughter protected… You are crossing a very fine line and you could lose her due to your actions.”

A third concerned fan fired: “She’s too young for that.”

Farrah’s mom Debra Danielsen came to their defense.

She previously told The Sun: “I don’t think it’s scandalous at all. We’ve always done funny little things like that around the house.”

Debra, 62, continued: “It’s not meant to be sexual. We live in a society where people love to hate and love to make a big deal out of nothing.

“They’re just having fun!”
