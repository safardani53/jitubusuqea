We all know the importance of a good clipper when it comes to keeping our acrylics in top-notch condition. To help, we’ve put together a guide to help you pick out a quality acrylic nail cutter. It’ll take you through the materials clippers are made of and how you can be better equipped when navigating this market.

What Are Good Acrylic Nail Clippers Made of?

Stainless steel

A fake nail trimmer made from stainless steel ensures sturdiness and guarantees longevity, thanks to its rust-proof quality. Steel acrylic trimmers will cost you a bit more, though, due to the nature of the material.

Plastic

Plastic is comparatively cheaper and affordable. While it won't be as durable as a steel nail cutter, plastic clippers for acrylics are extremely lightweight and provide a smooth grip. They'll also usually be available in a range of colors.

Things to Consider When Buying Acrylic Nail Clippers

Durability

Whether you are an expert nail artist or a beginner, no one likes a clipper that becomes squeaky and loose over time. This is why you must ensure the cutter you choose is made from durable and strong materials. You can get a good idea about the longevity of your new acrylic clipper by going through some customer testimonials.

Seamless operation

A good acrylic nail clipper should have jaws that align perfectly and work seamlessly. This will ensure the clipper doesn't jam up during operation or hurt you.

Size and weight

Another important thing you must make note of is how easy to carry and store your acrylic nail clipper is. You can get a good idea of this by considering its dimensions and weight. What you should be looking for is something that won't take up too much space in your self-care pouch. It should also be lightweight enough to take on the road.

Additional Buying Tips

Reliable spring

We recommend buying an acrylic nail clipper that has a strong and reliable spring so that it doesn’t break while you’re cutting your nails, as this might end up hurting you. A good quality spring will also help your clipper operate more smoothly. You can get an idea of how reliable your new nail cutter's springs are by going through some customer reviews. 

Sharp edge

It might be obvious, but we'd like to remind you that your acrylic nail clipper must boast a strong and sharp blade. This will ensure you can effortlessly cut through your fake nails. 

How to Use an Acrylic Nail Clipper

Using an acrylic nail clipper is no rocket science. You simply pick it up and put your acrylic extension under the tip. Then, depending on your clipper type, you can tilt this tip to shape your nails. Once you're set on a length and shape, simply press the clipper and nip off the acrylic.
