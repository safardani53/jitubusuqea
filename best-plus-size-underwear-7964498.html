How to Pick the Right Plus Size Underwear

Size

As plus size stylist Ashley Full puts it, underwear is the starting point to every outfit. And when it comes to shopping for plus size underwear, size is crucial. Full explains that many brands are still learning what a true plus size scale is, resulting in products that often run small.

To avoid any sizing mishaps, she suggests looking for brands that showcase their underwear designs on different body types. “This will give you a better idea of how the item will fit, and having a plus size model is a good indicator that the brand used a plus size woman to fit test their designs,” Full says. "Not all brands do this, and it makes a huge difference when it comes to accurate sizing.” 

Support

Finding the right amount of support in underwear can be tricky, but it is possible. Just make sure you're getting the right amount of support by choosing undies with all the right features (elastic waistband and leg openings, comfortable fabric, etc.) before you hit buy. “It’s kind of like the tale of Goldilocks and the Three Bears,” Full explains. “You’ll want something that is not too stretchy and not too tight, but just right.” 

Quality

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How do you measure for plus size underwear?

    When it comes to plus size underwear, it's all about knowing your measurements, says Full. “Figure out your chest (for bras), waist and hip measurements to help you determine how the designer scaled when designing their underwear,”she explains. “With plus size, this is crucial, as XXL is not equivalent to a 2X.”

  • Is it better to buy underwear bigger or smaller?

    Because plus-size underwear options are still evolving, you may find pairs from some brands run small, explains Full. “So, if you're having trouble finding the correct size, or are between sizes, don’t feel discouraged to size up for a more comfortable fit."

  • What material are best for underwear?

    “Whether you are plus size or straight size, material preference is always subjective,” Full tells PEOPLE. “Numerous people encounter sensory difficulties with specific fabrics or struggle with tight waistbands, so you really need to find what works best for you.
